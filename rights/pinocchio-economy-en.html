Book Summary

A child with a sense of economy grows into an adult who realizes his or her dreams. Pinocchio, Let’s Go on a Trip with Economy explains fundamental economic principles through a fairytale, inviting children to approach the concept of money in an easy and engaging way.

In the story, Pinocchio, the young protagonist, one day discovers a pair of ruby shoes that he desperately wants. However, he soon realizes that his family cannot afford them. Faced with this reality, Pinocchio unexpectedly finds a way forward and sets out on a journey. During his travels, he meets various people, encounters different situations, and gradually develops a sense of economy that he had not yet possessed.

In the epilogue, the author explains why she decided to write this story. As a child, she once wondered about her family’s economic situation — whether they were rich, average, or poor — because she wanted to understand, from her own perspective, why her mother could not buy her certain toys. Yet, adults never explained it to her. They simply told her to endure.

This book begins from the belief that children no longer need to endure without understanding. Through Pinocchio, Let’s Go on a Trip with Economy, children can naturally learn the concepts of money and basic economic principles in an accessible and enjoyable way.

The author further reflects on her experience as a parent. Explaining money to her own child was not easy, and she often worried whether teaching economic concepts might cause her child to lose innocence. This book is the result of those 고민과 사유 — written with the hope of helping children develop a healthy sense of economy while preserving their sense of wonder.

Through Pinocchio’s exciting journey, children learn practical ideas such as recording their spending, understanding how banks work, and using money responsibly. The book offers a meaningful solution for parents and educators who struggle to explain economic concepts to children.

Designed for both children and adults, this book encourages discussion at home and in the classroom. Vivid and creative illustrations stimulate the imagination, making the journey with Pinocchio even more engaging and joyful. Readers may feel both emotional resonance and the pleasure of learning.

By the end of the book, children, parents, and teachers alike can share precious and happy memories — discovering together that learning about money can be thoughtful, warm, and meaningful.

Author & Illustrator

Mun Seongcheol lives as a father of Ssong and a writer. Motivated by the lack of children’s books on economics, he wrote Pinocchio, Let's Go on a Trip with Economy to help children understand money and economic principles in an accessible way.

He has authored Why Is There No Zuckerberg in Korea?, Power of Venturing, Late Bloomer CEO, It's O.K. Even If You Are Gloomy, and Bullet-proof Studies. His book Why Is There No Zuckerberg in Korea? was introduced at the 2019 Frankfurt International Book Fair.

  • Instructor on Korea TV Worknet and YTN
  • Representative of Santa Fruits Inc. and Youth Sea Food Inc.
  • Former Business Development Team, Hyundai Corporation
  • Former IT Solution Business Department, Samsung Electronics
  • B.A. in Business Administration, Yonsei University

Lee Aeyoung, also known as Hato, is an illustrator and drawing calligrapher. She authored Irresistible Watercolor Calligraphy (2016) and has worked on major F&B brand projects, public installations, and film calligraphy.

Her illustration works include Pinocchio, Let's Go on a Trip with Economy, Humanities in My Hand: The Door of Dream, Stories of Indians that Wind Tells, and other titles.
